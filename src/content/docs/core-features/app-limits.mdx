App Limits let you set a maximum daily allowance for any app on your device. When you approach or hit the limit, Curbox gives you a calm, non-punishing nudge.

---

## How Limits Work

Curbox tracks real *foreground* usage for each app — time spent with the app actually on screen. Background activity (music, navigation) is not counted.

Limits reset at **midnight local time** by default. You can change the reset time in **Settings → Limits → Daily reset time**.

---

## Creating a Limit

<Steps>

1. **Go to** **Curbox → App Limits** (the clock icon in the bottom nav).

2. **Tap the `+` button** in the top-right corner.

3. **Select an app** from the list. Apps are sorted by your recent usage, making it easy to find your biggest time-sinks.

4. **Set the daily limit**

   Drag the dial or type a duration. You can set limits as short as 5 minutes or as long as 6 hours.

5. **Choose a warning threshold** (optional)

   You'll receive a quiet notification when this percentage of your limit is used. Defaults to 80%.

6. **Tap "Save"**.

</Steps>

<Aside type="tip">
Start with limits that are slightly above your current usage — for example, if you spend 2 hours on Instagram daily, start with a 1h 45m limit. Aggressive limits set on day one are easy to override. Gradual reduction is more effective.
</Aside>

---

## Category Limits

Instead of limiting individual apps, you can limit an entire *category* — for example, all apps tagged as "Social" or "Entertainment".

Category limits apply to the combined usage across all apps in that category.

<Code
  code={`Category: Social (4 apps)
  ├── Instagram
  ├── Twitter / X
  ├── TikTok
  └── Reddit

Daily limit: 1 hour total across all 4 apps`}
  lang="text"
  title="Example: Social Category Limit"
/>

## What Happens When You Hit a Limit?

By default:

1. At **80%** of your limit → a quiet notification ("20 minutes left on YouTube").
2. At **100%** → the app shows the calm lock screen with a message and the option to snooze for 15 minutes (once per day, per app).

The snooze option exists because Curbox is not a prison. It respects your autonomy while making the choice *intentional* rather than mindless.

---

## Pausing and Deleting Limits

- **Pause** a limit temporarily (e.g., for a weekend) via the long-press context menu on any limit card.
- **Delete** a limit by swiping it left in the App Limits list.

<Aside type="note">
Paused limits are shown greyed-out with a "Paused" badge. Usage during a pause is still tracked in your analytics — it is simply not enforced.
</Aside>
