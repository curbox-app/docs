Colour is one of the most powerful tools apps use to keep you engaged. Bright reds on notification badges, vivid thumbnails, saturated gradients — all of these trigger small dopamine responses that nudge you to keep looking. Curbox's Grayscale Filter removes this advantage by rendering selected apps entirely in black and white.

The effect is subtle but surprisingly powerful. An app that looks dull and grey is much easier to put down than one that is visually vibrant and stimulating.


## How It Works

Curbox monitors which app is in the foreground. When you open an app that belongs to a Grayscale Group, a full-screen grayscale overlay is placed over the display. The app still works normally underneath — you can read, tap, and scroll — but everything looks washed out.

When you leave the app, the overlay is removed and your screen returns to full colour immediately.

<Aside type="note">
The grayscale effect covers the entire screen, not just the app window. System UI elements like the status bar and navigation bar also appear in grayscale while the overlay is active.
</Aside>


## Grayscale Groups

Just like the App Blocker, the Grayscale Filter is organised into groups. A group is a named set of apps that share the same schedule. You might create:

- A **Social Media** group (Instagram, TikTok, X) active all day
- A **Work Hours** group (Slack, Gmail) active Monday to Friday during office hours to remind yourself not to doomscroll work apps on your phone
- A **Night Mode** group that activates after 9 PM to wind you down before bed


## Setting Up a Grayscale Group

<Steps>
1. **Navigate to Reducers**
   Open Curbox and tap **Reducers** in the bottom navigation bar.

2. **Open Grayscale**
   Find the **Grayscale** card under Anti-Stimulants and tap it.

3. **Add a new group**
   Tap the **Add** button (the plus icon in the bottom right).

4. **Name your group**
   Give it a descriptive name so you can identify it later.

5. **Select apps**
   Tap each app you want to include. All selected apps will share the same schedule.

6. **Set a schedule**
   Choose which days the grayscale should be active, then set the time window for each day. If you want it active all day every day, select all days and set the window from midnight to midnight.

7. **Save**
   Tap **Save**. The group is now active and Curbox will apply grayscale automatically according to your schedule.
</Steps>


## Tips for Getting the Most From Grayscale

**Pair it with the App Blocker.** Grayscale alone does not stop you from using an app — it just makes it less appealing. For apps you truly want to limit, combine a Grayscale Group with an App Blocker group. The grayscale makes the app feel less rewarding; the time limit enforces a hard cap.

**Start with your most-used apps.** Check your Usage Stats to find the apps consuming the most of your time, and add those to a grayscale group first.

**Give it a week.** The visual effect can feel jarring at first. Most people report that after a week their brain associates the grey look with "this app is boring" — which is exactly the goal.
