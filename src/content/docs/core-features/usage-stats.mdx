You cannot manage what you cannot measure. Curbox's Usage Stats screen shows you precisely how you are spending your time on your phone — broken down by app, by website, and by day — so you can make informed decisions about where to apply blocks and limits.


## The Usage Screen

Tap the **Usage** icon in the bottom navigation bar to open the stats dashboard.

The main view shows a list of all your apps sorted by the time you have spent in each one today. Each row displays the app name, icon, and total usage time. Tap any app to see a more detailed breakdown.


## App Usage Breakdown

Tapping an app opens its individual stats page, which includes:

- **Today's total usage** — the full time spent in this app since midnight
- **Daily usage chart** — a bar chart showing usage for each of the last seven days, so you can spot patterns (for example, does usage spike on weekends?)
- **Session history** — each individual time you opened the app today, with the start time and duration of each session

This level of detail helps you understand not just *how much* time you are spending, but *when* and in what patterns.


## Website Usage Tracking

Curbox also tracks time spent on individual websites inside supported browsers. This shows up as a separate section in the usage stats, broken down by domain.

For example, rather than just seeing "Chrome: 2 hours," you might see:

| Website | Time today |
|---|---|
| reddit.com | 48 minutes |
| youtube.com | 31 minutes |
| news.ycombinator.com | 12 minutes |

Supported browsers for website tracking include Chrome, Brave, and Vanadium.


## Filtering and Ignoring Apps

Some apps are irrelevant to your screen time goals — system utilities, launchers, or tools you use deliberately. You can exclude these from the stats to keep the view clean and focused on the apps that actually matter.

<Steps>
1. Open the **Usage** screen.
2. Tap the **Settings** or **Filter** icon.
3. Find the app you want to hide and add it to the **Ignored Apps** list.
</Steps>

Ignored apps still run normally; they are just excluded from your usage statistics.


## Home Screen Widget

Curbox provides a home screen widget that displays your current daily screen time at a glance. Adding it to your home screen means you see your usage total every time you pick up your phone — a constant, low-friction reality check without having to open the app.

To add the widget:

<Steps>
1. Long-press an empty area of your home screen.
2. Tap **Widgets**.
3. Find **Curbox** in the widget list.
4. Drag the widget to your desired position.
</Steps>

<Aside type="tip">
Place the widget on the first page of your home screen, ideally near the top. The more prominently it is positioned, the more often you will see it before reaching for a distracting app.
</Aside>


## Using Stats to Set Better Limits

The most effective way to use the Usage screen is as a diagnostic tool before configuring the App Blocker:

1. Watch your unmodified usage for a few days to get an honest baseline.
2. Identify the two or three apps consuming the most time that you did not intentionally plan to use.
3. Set App Blocker limits for those apps based on the actual average you observed.
4. Check back weekly to see if the limits are working and adjust as needed.

Starting from real data produces limits that are challenging but achievable, rather than arbitrary numbers that are either too easy to hit or so restrictive you immediately disable the blocker.
