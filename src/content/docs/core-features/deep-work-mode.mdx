Deep Work Mode is Curbox's most powerful feature. During an active session, selected apps are fully blocked — you cannot open them, and there is no snooze. It is designed to help you achieve the kind of sustained, distraction-free focus that produces your best work.

The name is inspired by Cal Newport's book *Deep Work*, which argues that the ability to focus without distraction is one of the most valuable skills in the modern economy — and one of the rarest.

---

## How It Differs from App Limits

| Feature | App Limits | Deep Work Mode |
|---------|-----------|----------------|
| Scope | Daily total usage cap | Time-boxed focus block |
| Enforcement | Soft (snooze available) | Hard (no snooze during session) |
| Duration | Resets daily | Active while session runs |
| Best for | Building daily habits | Uninterrupted work sprints |

---

## Starting a Deep Work Session

<Steps>

1. **Open Curbox** and tap the **"Deep Work"** tab (the lightning bolt icon).

2. **Set your duration**

   Choose from presets (25 min, 50 min, 90 min) or use the custom dial. Sessions can run up to 4 hours.

3. **Select apps to block**

   Curbox shows your high-usage apps pre-selected. You can add or remove apps from the block list.

   <Aside type="tip">
   Be thoughtful about what you *allow*. If you need a music app or a reference browser tab for your work, leave those unblocked.
   </Aside>

4. **Optionally, write your intention**

   Curbox lets you type a short note ("Finishing chapter 3") that is displayed on the lock screen as a reminder of *why* you started the session. Research shows that implementation intentions significantly increase follow-through.

5. **Tap "Begin Deep Work"** and confirm.

</Steps>

---

## During the Session

- Attempting to open a blocked app shows a full-screen calm reminder with your intention text and a countdown.
- The notification bar shows a persistent indicator of your active session.
- **Emergency exit** is available but requires typing a short confirmation phrase — making it a deliberate, conscious choice rather than an impulsive tap.

<Aside type="caution">
The emergency exit is intentionally inconvenient. This is a feature, not a bug. If you find yourself using it often, consider shortening your session duration rather than relying on exits.
</Aside>

---

## Scheduling Recurring Sessions

You can schedule Deep Work sessions to start automatically:

**Deep Work → Schedule → New schedule**

Scheduled sessions will automatically start at the configured time and send you a reminder 5 minutes beforehand.
