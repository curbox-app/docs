Curbox's analytics screen gives you an honest, non-judgmental picture of how you spend time on your device. The goal is *awareness*, not shame.

---

## The Daily Overview

The main analytics screen shows a 24-hour timeline of your device usage. Each app is shown as a coloured segment proportional to its share of your total screentime.

Below the timeline, apps are listed in order of usage, with:

- **Total time** used today
- **Sessions** — how many separate times you opened the app
- **Average session length** — often more revealing than total time

<Aside type="tip">
A high *number of sessions* with a short *average session length* is a classic sign of compulsive checking — the kind of usage that is most draining. Curbox surfaces this pattern clearly.
</Aside>

---

## Weekly and Monthly Trends

Swipe left on the overview to see 7-day and 30-day summaries. The weekly view shows:

- Total daily screentime bars (tap any bar for a breakdown)
- Your most-used app each day
- Comparison against your personal weekly average

---

## Focus Score

The **Focus Score** (0–100) is Curbox's composite metric. It weighs:

- Time spent in Deep Work sessions (positive)
- Limit violations / snooze usage (negative)
- Long, uninterrupted sessions vs. fragmented checking (positive vs. negative)

The score is personal — it is calibrated against *your own* baseline, not a universal standard. A score of 72 for you means something different than a score of 72 for someone else.

<Aside type="note">
The Focus Score algorithm is fully documented in the [source code](https://github.com/curbox-app/curbox). You can audit exactly how it is calculated.
</Aside>

## Exporting Your Data

All analytics data can be exported as **JSON** or **CSV** from:

**Analytics → ⋮ Menu → Export data**

The export includes raw session-level data. You can import it into your own scripts, spreadsheets, or visualisation tools.

---

## Deleting Your Data

To delete all stored analytics:

**Settings → Privacy → Manage my data → Delete all analytics**

This action is irreversible. Curbox will ask for confirmation and there is a 24-hour undo window.
