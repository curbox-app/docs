Now that Curbox is set up, let's start your first focused session. The whole process takes under a minute.

---

## What is a Focused Session?

A focused session is a time-boxed period during which you designate certain apps as *blocked*. While the session is active, those apps will show a calm, full-screen reminder instead of opening — keeping you anchored to your intention.

Sessions can run from **5 minutes to 8 hours**. They can be:

- **Timed** — automatically end after your chosen duration.
- **Manual** — you end them when you're done (requires a short cool-down to prevent impulsive exits).

---

## Starting a Session

<Steps>

1. **Open Curbox** and tap the large **"Start Focus"** button on the home screen.

2. **Choose a duration**

   Use the dial to pick a duration. The dial snaps to 5-minute increments. Common presets (25 min, 45 min, 90 min) appear as quick-select chips.

   <Aside type="tip">
   Research on sustained attention suggests 90 minutes as the natural limit of a deep work block. For beginners, 25-minute Pomodoro sessions are a great starting point.
   </Aside>

3. **Select apps to block**

   Curbox pre-selects apps it detected as high-usage from your weekly analytics. Review the list and adjust as needed.

4. **Tap "Begin Session"**

   A soft confirmation screen appears. Take a breath. Tap confirm.

5. **Curbox steps back**

   Curbox minimises itself. A persistent notification shows the remaining time. Attempting to open a blocked app will show a calm lock screen with your session timer.

</Steps>

---

## During a Session

- The **lock screen** displayed on blocked apps shows your remaining time and a short affirmation.
- You can always reach Curbox itself or allow-listed apps.
- To end early, go to Curbox → tap **"End Session"** → confirm (there is a 30-second cooldown to prevent impulsive exits).

<Aside type="caution">
If you are using Accessibility Service mode (not Shizuku), a determined user can still disable the service from Settings. Shizuku-powered blocking is significantly more robust. See the [Shizuku guide](/advanced/shizuku/).
</Aside>

---

## After a Session

When the timer runs out, Curbox shows a brief summary:

- Total focused time
- Number of times you tried to open a blocked app (a useful self-awareness metric)
- A gentle encouragement — never a guilt trip

Your session data is saved and visible in [Your Analytics →](/core-features/analytics/).
