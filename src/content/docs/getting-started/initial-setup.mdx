After installing Curbox, the first-launch setup wizard walks you through granting the necessary permissions. This page explains each permission and why it is required.

---

## Granting Permissions

<Steps>

1. **Usage Access (Required)**

   Curbox needs *Usage Access* to read which apps you have used and for how long. This is the only way to display accurate analytics and enforce time limits.

   When prompted, tap **Go to settings**, find **Curbox** in the list, and toggle *"Permit usage tracking"* on.

   <Aside type="caution">
   Without Usage Access, Curbox cannot function. If you skip this step, the app will remind you each time you open it.
   </Aside>

2. **Overlay / Draw over other apps (Required for blocking)**

   To show the calm lock screen when a time limit is reached, Curbox needs permission to draw over other apps.

   Tap **Go to settings**, find **Curbox**, and enable *"Allow display over other apps"*.

3. **Notification Access (Recommended)**

   This allows Curbox to give you gentle pre-limit warnings ("You have 10 minutes left on Instagram today").

   Tap **Allow** on the notification permission dialog. You can adjust notification behaviour at any time in your device settings.

4. **Accessibility Service (Optional — for Deep Work Mode)**

   [Deep Work Mode](/core-features/deep-work-mode/) uses an Accessibility Service to prevent you from force-quitting blocked apps. This is entirely opt-in and can be disabled when not needed.

   <Aside type="tip">
   If you are using [Shizuku](/advanced/shizuku/), you can replace the Accessibility Service with a more robust, privacy-preserving alternative. See the [Shizuku guide](/advanced/shizuku/) for details.
   </Aside>

</Steps>

## Privacy Dashboard

All data Curbox collects stays on your device. You can review, export, or delete it at any time from:

**Curbox → Settings → Privacy → Manage my data**

---

## Choosing a Default Mode

Curbox operates in two main modes:

| Mode | Best for |
|------|----------|
| **Standard** | Day-to-day app limits with soft warnings |
| **Deep Work** | Distraction-free focus blocks — apps are fully locked |

You can switch modes at any time. We recommend starting with **Standard** to get a feel for your usage patterns before adding hard blocks.
