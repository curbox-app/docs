Mindful Messages places a small, always-visible overlay on top of selected apps while you are using them. You write the message; Curbox keeps it in front of you. It is a soft, persistent reminder of your intentions — and it can include live data about how long you have already spent on the app today.


## Why It Works

Hard blocks are great for apps you want to avoid entirely. But for apps you use legitimately — messaging apps, email, even social media in moderation — a total block is not the right tool. Mindful Messages sits in the middle: it does not stop you from using the app, but it makes you continuously aware of the choice you are making and how long you have been making it.

Seeing "You've been on Instagram for 47 minutes today" while you are still scrolling is the kind of friction that prompts a real decision rather than mindless continuation.


## Dynamic Placeholders

Your message can include live data using placeholders. Curbox replaces them in real time as you use the app:

| Placeholder | What it shows |
|---|---|
| `{app_usage_today}` | Total time spent in this specific app today |
| `{screentime_today}` | Your total screen time across all apps today |
| `{live_session_duration}` | How long you have been in this app continuously since you opened it |

You can combine free text and placeholders however you like. The default message is:

```
Is this really important?
App Usage: {app_usage_today}
Screen Time: {screentime_today}
Session: {live_session_duration}
```

Feel free to replace or extend this with something more personal.

<Aside type="tip">
Short, direct messages work better than long ones. Something like "Worth it?" or "What were you going to do?" is more likely to create a pause than a paragraph of self-help text.
</Aside>


## Intent Log Integration

When you unlock a blocked app and choose the **Intent Log** unlock method, your stated reason is automatically piped into Mindful Messages for the duration of the unlock window. The overlay will show:

```
Your Intent:
I need to check the work message from Sarah

Time used today: {app_usage_today}
```

This keeps your own words visible while you use the app, making it easy to notice when you have drifted away from your stated purpose.


## Setting Up Mindful Messages

<Steps>
1. **Navigate to Reducers**
   Open Curbox and tap **Reducers** in the bottom navigation bar.

2. **Open Mindful Messages**
   Find the **Mindful Messages** card under Anti-Stimulants and tap it.

3. **Enable the feature**
   Toggle the main switch on.

4. **Select apps**
   Tap **Choose Apps** and select every app where you want the overlay to appear.

5. **Write your message**
   Edit the message field. Use the placeholders from the table above anywhere in your text.

6. **Choose the position**
   Select whether you want the overlay at the **Top** or **Bottom** of the screen. Top works well for most apps; Bottom can be better for video apps where the top of the screen is full of content.

7. **Save**
   Tap **Save**. The overlay will now appear whenever you open one of your selected apps.
</Steps>


## Overlay vs. Block

Mindful Messages is intentionally non-blocking. It sits on top of the app's content but does not intercept your taps or prevent you from using the app. If you want a harder stop, use the App Blocker instead. The two features complement each other well: Mindful Messages for the apps you are allowed to use, the App Blocker for the ones you genuinely want to cut off.
